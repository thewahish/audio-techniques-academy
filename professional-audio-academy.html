Professional Audio Engineering Reference

Fundamental Concepts

Frequency (Hz)
Number of sound wave cycles per second, measured in Hertz. Determines pitch: low frequencies (20-200 Hz) produce bass, mid frequencies (200-2000 Hz) carry speech clarity, high frequencies (2000-20000 Hz) provide brightness and definition.
Amplitude
Strength or intensity of a sound wave, directly related to perceived loudness. Measured in decibels (dB). Professional audio systems must maintain proper amplitude levels for optimal clarity and equipment protection.
Dynamic Range
Difference between the loudest and quietest sounds in an audio system, measured in dB. Professional systems require sufficient dynamic range to handle peak signals without distortion while maintaining quiet background noise levels.

Professional Equipment

Condenser Microphone
High-sensitivity microphone requiring phantom power (+48V). Ideal for detailed recording in controlled environments such as conference rooms, studios, and broadcast facilities.
Mixing Console
Central control interface for combining, routing, and processing multiple audio signals. Professional consoles feature EQ, dynamics processing, routing capabilities, and often include digital processing and automation features.
Line Array System
Speaker configuration using multiple drivers in vertical alignment for enhanced directional control and even coverage. Essential for large venues and government assembly halls.

Digital Audio Technology

Sample Rate
Number of digital samples captured per second during analog-to-digital conversion. Professional standards: 48kHz for broadcast/video, 44.1kHz for music, 96kHz+ for high-resolution applications.
Bit Depth
Number of bits used to represent each audio sample, determining available dynamic range. 16-bit provides 96dB range, 24-bit provides 144dB range. Professional recording typically uses 24-bit or higher.
Audio Network
Digital audio distribution system using Ethernet or specialized protocols like Dante, AVB, or CobraNet. Enables centralized control and distribution for large institutional installations.

Architectural Acoustics

Reverberation Time (RT60)
Time required for sound to decay by 60 decibels in a space. Critical for room design: speech rooms require ~0.6s, music venues ~1.8s, conference rooms ~0.8s.
Speech Intelligibility
Measure of spoken word clarity in a space, quantified by Speech Transmission Index (STI) from 0-1. Essential for government facilities where clear communication is critical.
Sound Masking
Controlled background sound used to reduce speech intelligibility in adjacent areas. Important for privacy in government offices and confidential meeting spaces.
