## **FOUNDATION LEVEL: ESSENTIAL SKILLS (Sessions 1-8)**

### **Session 1: Audio Physics & Acoustics Fundamentals**
**Learning Objective:** Master the physics of sound and acoustic principles that govern all audio work.

**Theory Components (1 hour):**
- Wave propagation, frequency, amplitude, and phase
- Room acoustics and reverberation
- Psychoacoustics and human hearing
- Digital sampling theory and Nyquist theorem

**Hands-On Lab (2 hours):**
- Sound wave analysis using spectrum analyzers
- Room measurement with Pink Noise and RTA
- Frequency response testing of speakers and rooms
- Digital conversion demonstrations (Sample rates, bit depth)

**Real-World Application:**
Students measure and analyze their own listening spaces, creating acoustic treatment plans.

**Equipment Required:**
- Spectrum analyzer software, Pink noise generator, SPL meter, Test microphones

---

### **Session 2: Digital Audio Principles & DAW Mastery**
**Learning Objective:** Comprehensive understanding of digital audio and Pro Tools proficiency.

**Theory Components (30 minutes):**
- Digital audio formats and codecs
- File management and session organization
- Backup strategies and version control

**Hands-On Workshop (2.5 hours):**
- Pro Tools certification-level training
- Session templates and workflow optimization
- Audio routing and I/O setup
- Keyboard shortcuts and productivity techniques

**Real-World Application:**
Students create professional session templates for different project types (music, podcast, film).

**Equipment Required:**
- Pro Tools Ultimate, Audio interfaces, MIDI controllers

---

### **Session 3: Microphone Technology & Placement**
**Learning Objective:** Expert-level microphone selection and positioning for any recording scenario.

**Theory Components (30 minutes):**
- Microphone types: Dynamic, Condenser, Ribbon
- Polar patterns and proximity effects
- Phantom power and impedance matching

**Hands-On Workshop (2.5 hours):**
- A/B testing of 10+ professional microphones
- Recording vocals with different mic techniques
- Drum kit miking with multiple approaches
- Stereo recording techniques (XY, ORTF, MS)

**Real-World Application:**
Students record a complete drum kit and vocal session, comparing techniques and documenting results.

**Equipment Required:**
- Variety of professional microphones (Shure, Neumann, AKG, Audio-Technica), Stands, Cables, Pop filters

---

### **Session 4: Signal Flow & Console Operations**
**Learning Objective:** Master analog and digital mixing console operation and signal routing.

**Theory Components (30 minutes):**
- Signal path from input to output
- Gain staging and headroom management
- Auxiliary sends and returns
- Insert points and patching

**Hands-On Workshop (2.5 hours):**
- Large format analog console session
- Digital mixer programming and recall
- Outboard gear integration
- Troubleshooting signal flow problems

**Real-World Application:**
Students wire and operate a complete recording session from scratch.

**Equipment Required:**
- Large format mixing console, Outboard processors, Patch bay, Digital mixer

---

### **Session 5: Recording Techniques & Session Management**
**Learning Objective:** Professional recording session management and advanced tracking techniques.

**Theory Components (30 minutes):**
- Session planning and preparation
- Communication with artists and producers
- Recording contracts and documentation
- File naming and organization standards

**Hands-On Workshop (2.5 hours):**
- Multi-musician tracking session
- Overdubbing and punch recording
- Headphone mixing and cue systems
- Session documentation and recall sheets

**Real-World Application:**
Students manage a complete band recording session with multiple musicians.

**Equipment Required:**
- Multi-channel headphone system, Talkback mic, Session documentation software

---

### **Session 6: Basic Editing & Audio Restoration**
**Learning Objective:** Professional audio editing and restoration techniques for damaged recordings.

**Theory Components (30 minutes):**
- Non-destructive editing principles
- Audio restoration algorithms
- Noise reduction techniques
- Declicking and spectral editing

**Hands-On Workshop (2.5 hours):**
- Editing podcast recordings and removing ums/ahs
- Restoring vintage recordings with noise and clicks
- Spectral editing for advanced cleanup
- Time stretching and pitch correction

**Real-World Application:**
Students restore damaged historical recordings and edit professional podcast episodes.

**Equipment Required:**
- iZotope RX Suite, Spectral editing software, Vintage recordings for practice

---

### **Session 7: Introduction to Mixing Philosophy**
**Learning Objective:** Understand the artistic and technical approach to professional mixing.

**Theory Components (30 minutes):**
- Mixing as an art form vs. technical process
- Reference monitoring and translation
- Mix preparation and organization
- Critical listening development

**Hands-On Workshop (2.5 hours):**
- Mix analysis of professional recordings
- Building basic static mixes
- Reference track comparison
- Monitor calibration and room tuning

**Real-World Application:**
Students create reference-quality mixes of provided multitrack sessions.

**Equipment Required:**
- Professional monitor speakers, Reference CDs, Mix analysis software

---

### **Session 8: Foundation Project - Complete Recording**

**Capstone Project (3.5 hours):**

## **Studio Specifications for Host Institutions**

### **Recording Studios (2 Studios Required)**
**Studio A - Large Format (25-40 students):**
- SSL or Neve large format console (48+ channels)
- Pro Tools Ultimate with 32+ I/O
- Professional monitor speakers (Genelec, Focal, ATC)
- Isolation booth with window
- Live room with variable acoustics
- Grand piano or high-quality digital piano

**Studio B - Control Room (15-25 students):**
- Mid-format digital console (Avid S6, SSL AWS)
- Pro Tools with sufficient I/O for class size
- Near-field monitors and headphone distribution
- Outboard processing rack
- Computer workstations for each student

### **Equipment Inventory (Minimum Requirements)**

**Microphones:**
- 4x Dynamic microphones (Shure SM57, SM58, Sennheiser MD421)
- 4x Large diaphragm condensers (Neumann U87, AKG C414, Audio-Technica AT4033)
- 2x Small diaphragm condensers (Neumann KM184, DPA 4011)
- 2x Ribbon microphones (Royer R-121, AEA R84)
- 1x Stereo microphone (Audio-Technica AT825)

**Monitoring:**
- Main monitors: Genelec or Focal professional monitors
- Near-field monitors: Yamaha NS-10M or equivalent
- Headphones: Sony MDR-7506, Audio-Technica ATH-M50x (20+ pairs)
- Headphone distribution amp

**Recording Equipment:**
- Audio interface with 16+ inputs/outputs
- Microphone preamps (API, Neve, or equivalent)
- Direct boxes (Radial, Countryman)
- Pop filters, shock mounts, microphone stands

**Outboard Processing:**
- Hardware compressors (1176, LA-2A style)
- EQ units (Pultec, API style)
- Reverb units (Lexicon, TC Electronic)
- Multi-effects processors

**Live Sound Equipment:**
- Portable PA system (line array or point source)
- Digital mixing console (Yamaha CL/QL, Allen & Heath)
- Wireless microphone systems (Shure, Sennheiser)
- System measurement tools (Smaart, SIM)
- Power amplifiers and speaker processors

**Computers & Software:**
- High-specification computers (Mac or PC)
- Pro Tools Ultimate licenses
- iZotope production suite
- Native Instruments Komplete
- Additional DAW licenses (Logic Pro, Cubase)

### **Classroom Requirements**

**Theory Classroom:**
- Capacity for 40 students
- Projection system for audio/video
- Individual audio workstations
- High-quality monitoring system
- Acoustic treatment for optimal listening

**Practice Rooms:**
- 6-8 small practice rooms with basic recording setup
- Individual workstations for homework and practice
- Headphone access and basic monitoring

### **Technical Support Requirements**

**Staffing:**
- Full-time technical coordinator
- Part-time assistant for equipment maintenance
- IT support for computer systems and networks

**Maintenance:**
- Regular calibration of monitoring systems
- Preventive maintenance schedule
- Backup equipment for critical components
