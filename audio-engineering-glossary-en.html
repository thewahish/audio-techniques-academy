üîä Basic Audio Concepts
Frequency (Hz)
The number of sound wave cycles per second, measured in Hertz. Determines pitch - low frequencies create bass sounds, high frequencies create treble.
Amplitude
The strength or intensity of a sound wave, directly related to volume. Higher amplitude equals louder sound.
Decibel (dB)
Unit of measurement for sound intensity. 0 dB is the threshold of hearing, 120 dB can cause hearing damage.
Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR)
The ratio between the desired audio signal and background noise. Higher SNR means cleaner, more professional sound.
üéöÔ∏è Mixing & Mastering
EQ (Equalizer)
Tool for adjusting the balance of different frequency ranges in audio. Essential for shaping tone and clarity.
Compression
Dynamic range control technique that reduces the difference between loud and quiet parts of audio for consistency.
Reverb
Acoustic effect that simulates the reflection of sound in different environments, adding depth and space to audio.
Panning
Positioning audio elements across the stereo field from left to right, creating width and separation in mixes.
üé§ Recording Technology
Condenser Microphone
Highly sensitive microphone type ideal for studio recording, requiring phantom power. Excellent for capturing detail.
Dynamic Microphone
Robust microphone type perfect for live sound and high-volume sources. No power required, very durable.
Phantom Power (+48V)
DC power supplied through microphone cables to operate condenser microphones and active DI boxes.
Audio Interface
Device that converts analog audio signals to digital for computer recording and digital back to analog for playback.
üîß Professional Equipment
Mixing Console
Central control surface for combining, routing, and processing multiple audio signals in live or studio environments.
Monitor Speakers
Specialized speakers designed for accurate audio reproduction in studios and control rooms for professional mixing.
DI Box (Direct Injection)
Device that converts high-impedance instrument signals to low-impedance microphone signals for mixing consoles.
Line Array
Speaker system configuration using multiple drivers in a line for enhanced directional control and coverage.
üì° Digital Audio
Sample Rate
Number of digital samples taken per second during analog-to-digital conversion. Standard rates: 44.1kHz, 48kHz, 96kHz.
Bit Depth
Number of bits used to represent each audio sample. Common depths: 16-bit, 24-bit, 32-bit. Higher = better quality.
Latency
Delay between audio input and output in digital systems. Critical for real-time monitoring and recording.
Plugin (VST/AU)
Software effects and instruments that integrate with Digital Audio Workstations for audio processing.
üèõÔ∏è Architectural Acoustics
Reverberation Time (RT60)
Time required for sound to decay by 60 decibels in a room. Critical measurement for acoustic design.
Sound Absorption
Process of converting sound energy to heat through materials, reducing reflections and controlling acoustics.
Diffusion
Scattering of sound waves to create even distribution throughout a space without dead spots.
Critical Distance
Point where direct sound from source equals reflected sound from room surfaces. Important for microphone placement.
