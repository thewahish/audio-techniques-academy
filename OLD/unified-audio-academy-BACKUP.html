Fundamental Concepts

Frequency (Hz)
Number of sound wave cycles per second, measured in Hertz. Determines pitch: low frequencies (20-200 Hz) produce bass, mid frequencies (200-2000 Hz) carry speech clarity.
Dynamic Range
Difference between the loudest and quietest sounds in an audio system, measured in dB. Professional systems require sufficient dynamic range for peak signals without distortion.

Professional Equipment

Condenser Microphone
High-sensitivity microphone requiring phantom power (+48V). Ideal for detailed recording in controlled environments such as conference rooms and studios.
Line Array System
Speaker configuration using multiple drivers in vertical alignment for enhanced directional control and even coverage. Essential for large venues and government assembly halls.
üíª

Digital Audio Technology

Sample Rate
Number of digital samples captured per second during analog-to-digital conversion. Professional standards: 48kHz for broadcast/video, 44.1kHz for music.
Audio Network
Digital audio distribution system using Ethernet or specialized protocols like Dante, AVB. Enables centralized control for large institutional installations.
üèóÔ∏è

Architectural Acoustics

Reverberation Time (RT60)
Time required for sound to decay by 60 decibels in a space. Critical for room design: speech rooms require ~0.6s, conference rooms ~0.8s.
Speech Intelligibility
Measure of spoken word clarity in a space, quantified by Speech Transmission Index (STI). Essential for government facilities where clear communication is critical.
